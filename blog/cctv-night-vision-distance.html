1. Traditional Infrared (Black & White)

Traditional IR cameras use infrared LEDs to illuminate a scene. While effective in total darkness, they have significant drawbacks for identification.

The Technical Pros

  • Zero-Light Performance: Works in 0 Lux conditions (absolute darkness).
  • Discretion: IR LEDs are often invisible or have a faint red glow (850nm vs 940nm).
  • Contrast: High contrast for moving objects at a distance.

The Technical Cons

  • Loss of Detail: You cannot identify the color of a vehicle or clothing.
  • IR Reflection: Number plates and faces often "white out" if they are too close to the LEDs.
  • Spider Webs: IR attracts insects, leading to false alarms and blurred vision.

2. Full-Color Night Vision (ColorVu / Full-color)

Modern sensors with F1.0 apertures and "Warm Light" LEDs allow cameras to stay in color mode 24/7.

Why It's Superior for Identification

  1. Clothing ID: Identifying a "red hoodie" instead of a "grey hoodie" is the difference between an arrest and a closed case.
  2. Vehicle Tracking: Capturing the exact shade of a getaway car provides vital evidence for local police.
  3. Sensor Technology: Uses back-illuminated (BSI) sensors that are significantly more sensitive than standard chips.
